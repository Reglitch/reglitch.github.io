A Utility Post? Really?

No, not a utility post. Let's dispel once and for all with this fiction that I don't know what I'm doing. I know EXACTLY what I'm doing. I am undertaking a systematic effort to change this game, to make Mistweaver more like the rest of the healers. Joking aside, this post isn't looking strictly at utility, instead we're just looking at buttons that each specialization has that doesn't fall under typical healing spell selection. For some classes this gets a little blurred, but where that happens I'll make an argument as to why it falls outside that definition.

I firmly believe that there is a problem worth noting here, one that for reasons of ensuring variety and fun should be looked at. This problem is that baseline abilities provide an anchor for which to augment the specialization through other avenues such as set bonuses, or artifact traits. Both of which look very boring for Mistweaver, I'm not sure it's a coincidence.

In this post I will list each class' spells, the criteria of which is as follows:

  • Isn't typically used during regular spell selection, i.e. not a standard single target or AoE spell.
  • Isn't the specialization's standard raid cooldown.
  • Isn't the specialization's standard tank cooldown.
  • Is an ability that is unique and powerful.

The Zero Sum Game

There isn't a zero sum game, obviously World of Warcraft has a little more nuance than adding up ability numbers and declaring the lowest the worst. However I'd ask you to go look at the quality of an Artifact such as the Resto Shamans and compare it to a Mistweaver. Here's an example of two traits taken from similar locations in each weapon:

Obviously this is somewhat cherry picked, but if you look at the amount of plain static % increases to commonly used abilities in the Mistweaver artifact and compare it to the amount that exist in the Shaman artifact, you can clearly see that the extra design space afforded by more base abilities is being taken advantage of. Ultimately it's resulting in a far more fun looking set of traits, which tie shamans together in a way that the highly atomic kit of a Mistweaver just hasn't been able to benefit from. We can see players already shunning spells like Essence Font because it just is there with no real interactions, it's not building on a legacy spell like Spinning Crane Kick. There are no artifact traits that build a spell like Essence Font into something better and more interesting, and as a result abilities like Rising Thunder which bring radical gameplay changes on the back of a playstyle players are more aware of and have a legacy are proving to be better than the base spec design. The developers need to realize that if they want the emotional effect from Artifacts to be successful, and if they want the new Mistweaver to be enjoyable, they need to stop adding 5% buffs and mobility effects that are severely diminished by our already strong mobility. What we need to see is more base abilities, more artifact traits that hinge off these base abilities, and more talents which pivot off them to provide gameplay changes that are meaningful in the likes of Rising Thunder.
